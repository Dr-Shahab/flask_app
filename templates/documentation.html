Documentation

MolScore-PK is a Flask and RDKit-based molecular profiling server for early-stage compound prioritization. It combines physicochemical descriptors, rule-based assessment, medicinal chemistry alerts, scaffold-aware summarization, liability mapping, goal-based prioritization, and optimization-focused output to help CADD users make faster decisions.

What MolScore-PK is designed to do

MolScore-PK is not intended to replace docking, QSAR, MD simulation, ADMET platforms, or biological evaluation. Its role is to provide a fast, structured first-pass assessment of molecular quality before deeper computational or experimental work. The platform is most useful when you need to quickly compare compounds, remove obvious liabilities, identify balanced analogs, or prepare a prioritized shortlist.

MolScore-PK is particularly useful for hit prioritization, analog comparison, early medicinal chemistry review, and small-batch compound profiling.
Supported input modes

Single SMILES

Use this mode for rapid profiling of one structure at a time. Best for focused review or quick checking of a newly proposed analog.

Batch SMILES

Paste multiple SMILES strings, one per line, to compare analogs side by side. This mode is useful for med-chem prioritization and small-series review.

Draw Molecule

Use the embedded JSME editor to sketch a structure, then submit the generated SMILES directly for analysis.

CSV Upload

Upload a CSV file containing a column named smiles. Up to 50 molecules are processed in one run.

CSV input is best when you want to compare many compounds using the same output workflow and export a structured result file afterward.
Project goal modes

MolScore-PK supports goal-aware ranking. The same molecule may score differently depending on the design context, which makes the platform more useful than a single universal score.

General

Balanced prioritization for broad early-stage screening without strong project-specific bias.

Oral

Favors balanced size, lipophilicity, polarity, absorption potential, and tractable developability.

Lead

Useful for analog refinement. Emphasizes tractable size, medicinal chemistry friendliness, and property balance.

Fragment

Prioritizes smaller, simpler compounds that are closer to fragment-like property space.

CNS

Favors lower polarity, limited hydrogen-bond burden, and BBB-friendly property space.

Goal-based ranking in MolScore-PK is a prioritization aid, not a claim of true oral bioavailability, brain exposure, or clinical suitability.

Key result panels and how to read them
Rapid Decision Summary

This block highlights the most important first-pass fields: ranking score, ranking class, project goal, bioavailability score, and Murcko scaffold. It is intended for quick shortlist review.

Liability Map

The liability map converts several important axes into green, amber, or red signals. This helps users detect whether solubility, lipophilicity, flexibility, alert burden, polarity, or synthetic accessibility needs attention.

Optimization Suggestions

This panel does not simply describe a molecule. It proposes practical next-step ideas, such as reducing lipophilicity, lowering TPSA, simplifying synthesis, or improving sp3 character.

Explainable Score Breakdown

The score breakdown panel shows how major property dimensions contribute to the final prioritization. This makes the ranking easier to understand and less like a black-box number.

Method notes and interpretation

MolScore-PK relies primarily on RDKit-based descriptor calculation and rule-based interpretation. Additional medicinal chemistry and prioritization layers are built on top of these outputs to improve readability and utility.

  • Molecular descriptors: computed from RDKit and related cheminformatics routines.
  • PAINS and Brenk alerts: derived from RDKit filter catalogs.
  • Synthetic accessibility: estimated using the SA score method when available in the environment, with a fallback if not.
  • Murcko scaffold: used to summarize scaffold-level identity for analog-focused workflows.
  • Goal-aware ranking: uses project-specific weighting to prioritize compounds differently for general, oral, lead, fragment, and CNS modes.
Some displayed labels such as GI absorption or BBB-oriented favorability are screening-style interpretations based on property windows, not experimentally validated ADME predictions.
Batch analysis and export

When multiple valid molecules are submitted, MolScore-PK generates a batch summary that reports the number of valid structures, scaffold diversity, average ranking score, average molecular weight, average LogP, and the highest-ranked molecule in the set.

Results can be exported as CSV for downstream filtering, plotting, med-chem discussion, or supplementary analysis.

Limitations and good practice

Not a replacement for experiments

MolScore-PK does not replace biological testing, permeability experiments, solubility assays, or PK measurements.

Not a target-specific predictor

The server evaluates molecular quality and liabilities, but it does not predict target affinity or selectivity by itself.

Use interpretation carefully

This tools assist in compound prioritization while leaving final decisions to medicinal chemistry expertise and project-specific context.

A compound with a poor score may still become valuable after optimization, and a compound with a good score may still fail later for potency, selectivity, toxicity, or PK reasons.
Recommended use workflow
  1. Submit one molecule or a small batch of analogs.
  2. Choose a project goal that matches the intended design context.
  3. Review the rapid summary and liability map first.
  4. Use medicinal chemistry alerts and scaffold information to identify obvious risks.
  5. Read the optimization suggestions before deciding which compounds to keep, redesign, or deprioritize.
  6. Export the results if you want to compare compounds outside the server.
For practical CADD workflows, MolScore-PK works best as a screening and prioritization layer before docking, QSAR model building, synthesis planning, or experimental progression.
